Variation in relationships, distributions, or model parameters across space.

## Definition

Spatial heterogeneity exists when the relationship between `Y` and `X` is not constant across locations. A predictor may have a positive effect in one region and a weaker, stronger, or opposite effect elsewhere.

## Modeling Relevance

Spatial heterogeneity motivates local or varying-coefficient estimators such as [[mgwr]], [[svc]], [[stvc]], and [[mgwrsar]].
